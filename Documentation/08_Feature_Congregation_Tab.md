# Feature: The Congregation Tab

> **Surfaces:** Tier A only (iOS, Android, Mac, Windows). Elders and secretaries often use **Mac** or **Windows** for roster work at a desk.

The 4th tab in the main navigation is labeled **"Congregation"** (or shortened to **"Cong."** on smaller mobile screens to prevent text wrapping). This tab replaces the old, cold "Directory" concept with a modern, structured community view.

## 1. The Main Views
The tab features a simple toggle at the top of the screen to switch between two primary views:
- **All Publishers (A-Z):** A clean, alphabetical list of everyone in the congregation, functioning like a highly polished native contacts app.
- **Service Groups:** A structured, card-based view that organizes publishers into their assigned Field Service Groups.

## 2. Service Group Structure
When viewing the Service Groups, the UI instantly conveys the hierarchy and leadership of each group:
- **Group Overseer:** Prominently highlighted at the top of the group card with a distinct "Overseer" badge.
- **Assistant(s):** Listed immediately below the Overseer with an "Assistant" badge.
- **Group Members:** The remaining publishers assigned to that specific group.

## 3. Publisher Badges & Privileges
To quickly convey a publisher's role without requiring the user to click into their full profile, the list uses subtle, colorful badges next to their names:
- **Pioneer:** A distinct icon (e.g., a small star or briefcase) indicating they are in the full-time ministry.
- **Elder / Ministerial Servant:** Subtle indicators for appointed men.
- *(Note: To maintain privacy, certain badges or contact details may be hidden from regular publishers depending on the congregation's specific privacy configuration, but Elders will always see all badges).*

## 4. Quick Actions
Tapping on a publisher's name slides up a beautiful quick-action card allowing the user to instantly:
- Call
- WhatsApp Message
- Email
*(This massively adds value to the app, entirely removing the need for publishers to manually save dozens of numbers into their personal phone books).*
